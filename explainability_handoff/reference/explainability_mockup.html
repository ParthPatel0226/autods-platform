🧠 In plain English

Your model relies most heavily on comorbidity burden (Charlson score) — patients with 3+ comorbidities are nearly 4× more likely to be flagged for readmission. Length of stay is the second-strongest driver: each additional day beyond the median increases predicted risk by ~6%. Prior admission history acts as a multiplier — patients with 2+ prior admissions in the last year have systematically elevated scores regardless of other factors. Interestingly, BMI and gender have minimal independent effects, suggesting the model avoids demographic shortcuts. The discharge disposition is the only feature that substantially reduces predicted risk — patients discharged to home with follow-up care show 35% lower predicted readmission rates.

🔗 Key interactions

The strongest interaction is between charlson_score × length_of_stay — high comorbidity patients who also have long stays are penalized far more than either factor alone would predict. Prior admissions × age interact synergistically: elderly patients with readmission history receive disproportionately higher risk scores. Interestingly, hba1c × num_medications shows a moderate interaction — uncontrolled diabetes (high HbA1c) combined with polypharmacy creates a compounding risk signal the model has learned to detect.

⚖ Fairness summary

The model passes the 4/5ths (80%) disparate impact test across gender groups — no group receives a substantially different positive prediction rate. Equal opportunity shows a marginal gap of 6% between the youngest and oldest age groups: the model is slightly less sensitive (lower true positive rate) for patients 65+, likely because their baseline readmission rate is higher, compressing the margin. Predictive parity across race is strong at 3% — precision is consistent regardless of racial group. Overall, the model meets HIPAA-aware fairness thresholds, though the age-related gap warrants monitoring in production.
